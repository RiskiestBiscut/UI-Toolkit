Heuristic Evaluation

In terms of actually prototyping an idea it is hard to nail down a specific method because it really depends on what is being prototyped! It could be a simple paper prototype, something digital, or something physical. However, Heuristic evaluation can be implemented throughout the prototyping process to make it the best prototype it can be. The goal of heuristic evalutaiton is to implement all people in a team to evaluate to product based on a set of ‘best-practices’.

How To

Heuristic evaluation is less of a step-by-step process and more a list of criteria for all members of a team to evaluate a design by throughout the prototyping process. In order to perform a proper heuristic evaluation the team needs to have a common knowledge base.. Jakob Nielsen has a list of 10 heuristics to follow when performing heuristic evaluation:

  1. Visibility of system status
  2. Match between system and the real world
  3. User control freedom
  4. Consistency and standards
  5. Error prevention
  6. Recognition rather than recall
  7. Flexibility and efficiency of use
  8. Aesthetics and minimalist design
  9. Help users recognize, diagnose, and recover from errors
  10. Help and documentation

Having all team members evaluate a prototype against these criteria throughout the prototyping process will help to ensure that many usability issues are discovered and corrected before the testing phase. A playlist explaining each heuristic can be found here.
